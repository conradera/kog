What's KOG

This Website shall enable you to learn one or two things about your mind and how best you can guard it The first few times I heard about mental health disorders and how important it is to ensure your mind is healthy, and a lot of talk about depression and how common it is these days, I just thought people had gone soft but with time, I started identifying people around me actually losing their sanity and I continued to convince myself it was because all the information had forced me to acknowledge their existence and because of that I had started labelling occasional sadness and cold feet as depression and anxiety, I convinced myself. Luckily, I am not the first person to have a painful experience relevant to what I ridiculed, just think about all those formerly rich people who thought they would never be poor, or those couples in which the individuals thought they would never be apart, another example would be the Manchester United fans who thought the team they support would be in the bottom half of the premiere league table. Just like those groups of people, I thought I could never have a mental health disorder. After what occurred in the last third of 2024, I realize that perhaps God not acknowledging our "boundaries" is just a way of Him reminding us who's boss.

"Becoming aware is painful, doing something about it is difficult."

The first time I talked to a professional, I was in desperate need of pain relief, if I were on a mission it would be titled “Mission No Pain After Session”. After the session, I was far from saying, “Mission accomplished!”.
I may have actually been thinking “This is a mission impossible.”

Now a lady was sitting across me, there was food, so I ate as she prepared, and as I was chewing, I thought I love therapy already" but it was time to talk, actually say words and she asks and I answer, at first it seemed like a smooth ride, until it suddenly felt like I was bouncing in potholes while navigating Kampala, I was in tears and she was looking at me with this immense pity and I was regretting my choices, wondering why she was asking so many questions, so many hard questions, I came here for the pain to go away not to keep the pain and get homework a long side it.

A few sessions later I realize therapy is not really a pain relief center, actually it could increase the pain in earlier parts, it's purpose is to enable you understand yourself more and why you feel the way you do in certain situations. It can be incredibly painful at first but professionals exist to help navigate that.

Going through such steps to acknowledge your emotions actually protects the people around you, preventing you from having dramatic breakdowns that could bruise those you care about, and hurt people hurt people so you could prevent that cycle.

You don't have to go to a professional, when you're insanely angry or deeply sad are you hungry, lonely or tired? Have you talked to a specific person? Do you feel jealous? If you know what goes on within yourself, you can prevent it other than going around tearing hearts bit by bit and turning into a communal problem due to something that could be so slight.The process is difficult and takes some time but it's not impossible.
