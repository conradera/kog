DOPAMINE

Whats this?

When you're hot your body produces sweat to cool you down, this enables your body temperature to remain in a state of balance. Now imagine if your normal sweat was replaced by ice or if your body started freezing every time you got hot. The body temperature would be far too low. you would need to do something to increase your body temperature again, and more of it this time, since youâ€™re temperature would be below the normal, but when you get it back up, you would freeze again and have to re-do something to get your temperature back up and the cycle would go on. The ice sweat example could be a metaphorical way of describing what happens in your brain during addiction. Every time you eat, watch a movie, a funny video, take alcohol or smoke, your brain produces dopamine which is the reason why after these activities you feel happy, motivated and rewarded.

Dopamine in this case is like the heat. Although your body's state of balance must be maintained so the brain tries to do this by producing grimbles which could be compared to sweat, although unlike sweat (the normal one) they do not make your emotions neutral, like it would make your body temperature constant.

Grimbles are like ice sweat they would make you feel slightly sadder or tired, that's the reason why after taking alcohol and soaking into the ecstasy of a drunkard state, one gets a hangover. Since grimbles cause slight pain, a person is forced to use the source of happiness awaken a state of pleasure which only leads to more grimbles and the cycle goes on causing addiction. With access to pleasure all the time; on our phones, television, food, drugs. Within a lot of people grimbles are over activated causing pain, dissatisfaction and demotivation which explains why cases of sloth and depression are higher than they have ever been.

HUMAN PILLS

understand the human pill

A lot of peopleâ€™s perception of addiction originates from an experiment carried out in the mid1950s by Dr. James Olds. In this study, a rat was placed in a cage and had unlimited access to two water bottles, one with plain water and another laced with drugs specifically morphine. The rat tasted both the substances and eventually it preferred the water with morphine and took more of it, over time it overdosed and died.

Almost two decades later, Dr. Bruce Alexander thought the previously mentioned experiment was flawed since rats like humans are social creatures and therefore redesigned it, this time in a more spacious environment and with more rats, the rodents had access to food, toys, they were even reproducing. Again two bottles were available, one with plain water and the other with water containing morphine, the rats tasted both the substances although this time showed negligible interest in the drugged water and preferred the plain water even when the concentration of the drug was increased, they still did not become addicted.

The first experiment gives of the impression that addiction lies within chemical bonds whereas the second more clearly portrays it lies within our social bonds. Addictive substances arenâ€™t simple addictive by nature, your environment has to be bruised in one way or another before they have such a powerful effect on you
