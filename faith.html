Using the Cross as a Bridge Between the Spirit and the Mind
The first time I became curious about Christ’s crucifixion was in Primary Four when an English teacher said He was crucified naked. I wondered why that wasn’t taught in a church sermon.

I may not know how much you understand about Roman crucifixion, but now I see its meaning more clearly. As Easter approaches, what better time to reflect?

People often live in anxiety about what could go wrong, robbing them of peace. Yet Jesus, aware of His fate, still lived with purpose and shared hope with others.

I. Spiraling

“And being in agony, He prayed more earnestly. Then His sweat became like great drops of blood falling down to the ground.” – Luke 22:44 (NKJV)

Jesus suffered from a condition called hematohidrosis—sweating blood due to intense stress. His mental anguish was immense, yet He accepted His cup of suffering in obedience to God’s will.

II. Worth

Jesus was betrayed for 30 pieces of silver—the value of a slave. Still, He endured silently. Soldiers mocked and tortured Him, yet He didn’t resist or defend Himself before Pilate.

“So Pilate released Barabbas to them. He ordered Jesus flogged with a lead-tipped whip, then turned Him over to the Roman soldiers to be crucified.” – Matthew 27:26 (NLT)

The scourging tore flesh from His body, causing severe blood loss and thirst. A crown of thorns was forced onto His head, injuring facial nerves. The robe they used mocked Him, then was ripped off, reopening wounds.

Jesus couldn’t carry the cross alone—Simon of Cyrene was compelled to help, showing how we too often carry unexpected burdens.

Nails were driven into Jesus’ wrists to support His weight, causing nerve damage and extreme pain. Breathing became agonizing, requiring Him to push up on His nailed feet. Yet He still said,

"Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they are doing." – Luke 23:34

His heart was under immense physical and emotional stress. Scientists believe He died of a heart attack—a heart truly broken for humanity.

He endured all this for YOU. Though betrayed for a low price, He lifted your worth from worthless to priceless (Isaiah 52:14).
